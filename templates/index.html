Jonnathan Phillips

For office as Governor of Pennsylvania

Born and Raised in Pittsburgh, Jon Phillips has allways had a passion for education. Trained to be a teacher, Jon has worked in numerous schools around the city with children of all ages to young adults. In 2012 the Austin Academy of education, where Jon was working as an English teacher was hit by budget cuts and after two years struggling to raise funds, was forced to close, leaving thousands of children without local access to a school.

After taking part in numerous campaigns to try and save the Austin Academy, Jon's attention turned to the cause of the problem, the removal of the Academies funding. Now after years of campaigning Jon and his election team are ready to take the fight to Washington.

Show, don't tell.

One of the first things you learn when training to become a teacher is the following phrase: "Show, don't tell!"

The concept is simple enough, instead of trying to tell a student how something works, show them. In doing so, the student is allowed to clearly see what you are trying to teach, without added unessisary information or the tutors personal bias on the topic. I've allways had success in implementing this idea with my students and want to take the same approach with my politics.

The next section of this webpage has been added to do just that. My team has produced a "data dashboard" using data from the USA school donations database. This database contains up to date records of school funding donations to the different states of the USA. What the dashboard allows you to do is directly interact with the information which has been collected and clearly see what is going on.

Click on the tutorial button now for further information on how to manipulate the data dashboard in the next section.
